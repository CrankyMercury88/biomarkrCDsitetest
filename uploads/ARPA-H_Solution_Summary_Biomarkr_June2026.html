The Problem: Medicine is Blind to Immune Trajectories

Immune states are dynamic, personal, and trajectory-dependent. A snapshot IL-6 of 15 pg/mL means nothing without knowing whether it rose from 5 in six hours or has been stable at 15 for two weeks. Yet every existing diagnostic gives you a snapshot.

Chronic inflammatory conditions — rheumatoid arthritis, IBD, Long COVID, cardiovascular disease — affect over 100 million Americans and cost the healthcare system more than $800 billion annually. Despite this, clinicians manage these conditions with tools that are structurally mismatched to the biology:

❌ Current Reality

Cytokine testing requires a centralized lab, venipuncture, 24–72 hour turnaround, and costs $200–$800 per panel. Results arrive too late to act on. Most patients are never tested at all.

❌ The Snapshot Problem

Single-timepoint cytokine measurements cannot distinguish a rising flare from a resolving one, a treatment responder from a non-responder, or an early immune deviation from random noise.

❌ Population References Fail Individuals

Reference ranges are derived from population statistics. A patient whose IL-6 doubles from their personal baseline — while remaining within "normal" range — receives no clinical alert.

❌ Circadian Confounds Are Ignored

IL-6, TNF-α, and IL-1β follow strong circadian rhythms that vary 2–4-fold within a single day. No existing clinical protocol accounts for this, contaminating every cytokine measurement taken.

The clinical consequence: flares go undetected until they are severe, treatments are continued past the point of efficacy, and dose adjustments are based on symptom reports rather than biological evidence. This is a solvable engineering problem — not a scientific one. The biology is understood. The measurement infrastructure does not exist.

Q-SENS is not a diagnostic test in the traditional sense. It is a longitudinal immune monitoring platform — the cytokine equivalent of continuous glucose monitoring. Its value proposition is the trajectory, not the reading. Frequent, low-cost, multiplexed measurement at home enables three capabilities no existing technology provides:

1. Personalized baseline establishment — repeated measurement in health establishes each patient's individual circadian-calibrated cytokine baseline, against which future readings are interpreted as deviations, not absolute values.

2. Trajectory-based signal extraction — rate of change, time-to-peak, resolution velocity, and ratio dynamics encode information invisible to any single measurement.

3. Pre-symptomatic flare prediction — longitudinal ML models trained on personal trajectories can detect immune deviations days before clinical symptom onset.

Cytokine Trajectory Signatures: The Diagnostic Signal

The five Q-SENS cytokines — IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β, IFN-γ, IL-10 — span four functional immune axes: innate initiation, systemic amplification, adaptive effector function, and counter-regulation. Each disease condition produces a characteristic temporal signature in the relationships and trajectories of these markers, not in their absolute values. The charts below show simulated trajectories based on published clinical literature, illustrating the pattern discrimination that longitudinal monitoring enables.

Sepsis — Cytokine storm signature (hours)
TNF-α & IL-1β spike within hours. IL-6 rises rapidly and persists. IL-10 counter-regulatory surge. IFN-γ suppressed — immunoparalysis signature.
RA Flare — Pre-symptomatic trajectory (days)
IL-6 & TNF-α rise 3–5 days before clinical flare. IL-10 insufficient to suppress — explains breakthrough. IFN-γ shows modest Th1 involvement.
Long COVID — Persistent immune dysregulation (weeks)
IL-6 & IFN-γ elevated but variable — episodic flaring pattern. Blunted IL-10 response distinguishes from recovery. TNF-α smoldering chronically.

The critical insight is that these signatures are trajectory-dependent, not threshold-dependent. The same absolute IL-6 concentration carries opposite clinical meaning depending on whether it is rising, stable, or falling — and whether it represents a 2-fold or 10-fold deviation from that individual's personal baseline. No single-timepoint test, however sensitive, can extract this information.

04 —

The Circadian Problem — and Its Solution

A fundamental and largely ignored confound in cytokine measurement is the circadian rhythm of immune mediators. IL-6, TNF-α, and IL-1β vary 2–4-fold within a single 24-hour period, peaking in the early morning hours and nadir-ing in the afternoon. This means a morning IL-6 of 8 pg/mL and an afternoon IL-6 of 4 pg/mL in the same patient may represent identical immune states — or opposite ones.

Cytokine circadian rhythm — individual variation across a 24-hour cycle (illustrative)
Time axis normalized to individual wake time (T=0), not clock time. Peak-to-nadir amplitude varies substantially between individuals, making population-level correction ineffective.

The Q-SENS platform addresses this through a dedicated Circadian Characterization Phase at trial enrollment: participants test every 4 hours for 14 days, wake-time normalized (not clock-normalized), generating each individual's personal circadian cytokine profile. All subsequent trajectory analysis is phase-corrected against this personal map — a capability no existing inflammatory monitoring approach provides.

This is not a minor methodological refinement. Without circadian normalization, up to 40% of apparent cytokine "deviations" in a longitudinal dataset are circadian noise. With it, the signal-to-noise ratio for detecting true immune events improves by an estimated 3–5x, directly enabling the pre-symptomatic flare prediction that is the platform's central clinical claim.

Primary Use Cases — Where This Platform Changes Outcomes

Q-SENS is a horizontal platform. The following use cases represent the highest-priority clinical and research applications, each validated by an established unmet need and a clear cytokine biological rationale.

🦴
RA / Autoimmune
Biologic Treatment Monitoring & Flare Prediction

1.5M US RA patients on biologics have no objective home monitoring. Q-SENS trajectories detect treatment response within days of initiation and predict flares 5–7 days before clinical presentation, enabling dose adjustment before joint damage occurs.

🫁
Long COVID / ME-CFS
Objective Immune Phenotyping — No FDA Dx Exists

Over 17M Americans with Long COVID have no validated objective biomarker. Longitudinal cytokine trajectories — particularly IFN-γ and IL-6 episodic patterns — provide the first objective immune fingerprint of post-acute sequelae, enabling clinical trial stratification and treatment monitoring.

🫀
IBD
Treatment Response Monitoring Between Infusions

3M+ IBD patients on infliximab, vedolizumab, and ustekinumab are assessed clinically every 8–12 weeks. Q-SENS monitoring between visits detects secondary loss-of-response before clinical relapse, enabling proactive switching rather than reactive dose escalation.

💊
Pharma / DCT
Decentralized Clinical Trial Pharmacodynamics

Pharma trials on immune-modulating biologics measure pharmacodynamic effect at site visits every 4–12 weeks. Q-SENS provides continuous remote PD data between visits, compressing trial timelines and enabling proof-of-concept at lower N with higher confidence.

🔬
Research
Immune Baseline Population Studies

Academic researchers studying exercise physiology, nutritional interventions, sleep, aging, and vaccine response currently rely on ELISA (72-hour turnaround, single-plex) or Luminex (lab-bound). Q-SENS enables frequent, multi-site, standardized cytokine collection in ambulatory populations.

🏥
Proactive Health
Pre-Clinical Immune Surveillance in High-Risk Populations

Patients at elevated cardiovascular risk, cancer survivors, and post-surgical patients carry chronic low-grade inflammation invisible to standard-of-care labs. Longitudinal Q-SENS monitoring detects smoldering immune activation before organ damage, enabling early intervention at a fraction of downstream treatment cost.

Why This Is an ARPA-H Problem

The barriers to building a trajectory-aware immune monitoring platform are not scientific — the biology is understood, the cytokines are validated, the sensing technology exists. The barriers are engineering and clinical validation challenges that private capital cannot efficiently fund at this stage:

Private capital won't fund the circadian characterization study. Thirty healthy volunteers, 6x/day testing for 14 days, generating no revenue and no IP — but generating the normative dataset without which the trajectory algorithm is unvalidated. This is exactly the kind of enabling pre-competitive infrastructure ARPA-H is designed to fund.

The feasibility cohort requires longitudinal infrastructure before the commercial case is proven. No Series A investor will fund a 6-month, 60-patient monitoring study at this stage. But without this data, the large-scale government-funded trial needed to validate the clinical claim cannot be justified.

This is a platform bet, not a product bet. Q-SENS's 32-biomarker architecture means the cytokine panel validated here becomes the template for expansion to cardiovascular, oncology, neurology, and pandemic preparedness applications — exactly the kind of transformative platform impact that aligns with ARPA-H's mission to deliver health breakthroughs in years, not decades.

The CGM did not succeed because blood glucose was a new biomarker. It succeeded because frequent measurement transformed a snapshot into a trajectory — and that trajectory enabled entirely new clinical interventions. Q-SENS does the same for the immune system. ARPA-H can make this happen within 24 months.
